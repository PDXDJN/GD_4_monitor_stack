Module Concept: Inter-Monitor Space Jellyfish

A calm ambient visual module featuring a large, elegant space jellyfish rendered entirely in vector graphics, drifting slowly through the desktop environment as if it exists in a layer between the user’s monitors. The effect should feel more like a living screensaver creature than a cartoon mascot: quiet, graceful, slightly alien, and hypnotic.

The point is not “look at me.” The point is presence. It should make the monitor wall feel like a shared aquarium in orbit.

Core Visual Idea

The creature is a bioluminescent cosmic jellyfish with a semi-transparent bell and long trailing tendrils that extend far enough to cross monitor boundaries. The head or bell may be centered mostly on one display at a time, but the tentacles should often reach into neighboring screens, making the creature feel physically larger than any single monitor.

That cross-monitor continuity is the trick that sells the illusion. Otherwise it’s just desktop wallpaper with commitment issues.

The jellyfish should feel:

serene

weightless

non-threatening

majestic

slightly mysterious

unmistakably “space” rather than “ocean”

Not goofy. Not Pixar. Not cute-cute. More like a meditative alien lifeform.

Animation Behavior
Overall motion

The jellyfish should move very slowly, with a soft vertical bobbing motion:

gentle upward drift

slow pause

subtle downward settling

slight horizontal meander

gradual rotation or tilt by a few degrees at most

It should never zip, jerk, snap, or behave like it’s trying to win a boss fight.

The motion should feel like:

floating in zero gravity with some imagined current

breathing

drifting through a calm fluid medium

a slow ambient sculpture that happens to be alive

Swim cycle

The bell should contract and relax in a slow rhythm, creating the impression of propulsion without making the motion too energetic.

Suggested cadence:

contract over 1.5–2 seconds

release over 2–3 seconds

then a slight pause before the next pulse

This means a full cycle might take 4–6 seconds, maybe even longer. The bell movement should be subtle, not dramatic. Think meditative tai chi, not jellyfish on espresso.

Bobbing motion

In addition to the swim pulse, the entire creature should follow a very slow sine-wave vertical motion:

total vertical travel: modest, maybe 40–120 px depending on resolution and scale

cycle duration: around 10–20 seconds

never perfectly repetitive if possible; layer multiple small oscillations so it feels organic

Tentacle motion

The tentacles are where the soul of the thing lives.

They should:

trail behind the body with delayed motion

sway independently with slightly different phase offsets

bend in smooth arcs rather than sharp joints

keep a sense of drag, as though moving through a medium

continue drifting even when the bell is nearly still

Important: the tentacles should not all move together. That makes them look like noodles in a fan. Each one should have its own subtle timing and curvature.

Multi-Monitor Presence

This is one of the defining features.

Spatial design

The jellyfish’s bell might occupy one monitor primarily, but its full body should extend across multiple displays:

the bell may be centered on monitor A

major tentacles can trail down into monitor B

a few longer filaments may cross into monitor C

as it drifts, the center of mass can slowly migrate between screens

The module should feel like the creature is larger than the display system’s borders, not confined by them.

Visual continuity across bezels

The animation should treat the monitor array as one continuous coordinate space. Tentacles should not “restart” at screen edges. They should pass cleanly across boundaries, even if there is a physical bezel gap in real life.

This creates a lovely illusion that the jellyfish is behind or within the whole monitor wall.

Slow traversals

If horizontal movement is included, it should be extremely gradual:

crossing from one monitor center to another might take minutes

the user should feel like the jellyfish is simply drifting through its habitat

monitor transitions should feel incidental, not like pathfinding

Vector Graphic Style

Everything should be vector based, using clean scalable shapes and line work.

Bell / body

The bell should be built from layered vector shapes:

main dome shape, soft rounded silhouette

inner translucent layers for depth

thin glowing rim or edge highlight

faint inner structures suggesting alien anatomy

optional subtle starfield or nebula-like texture masks, but still vector-friendly

The bell should feel soft and luminous, with transparent overlap and depth.

Tentacles

Use multiple categories of appendages:

Primary tendrils

Long, elegant, ribbon-like lines that extend across monitors.

Secondary filaments

Finer threads branching from the main tentacles, moving even more gently.

Energy fronds

Optional soft glowing strands that feel less biological and more cosmic, like plasma or faint aurora trails.

The combination helps it read as “space jellyfish” rather than just “jellyfish somebody pasted onto a galaxy background.”

Line quality

Lines should be:

smooth

anti-aliased

slightly varying in thickness

tapered at the ends

softly glowing or feathered if supported

No harsh black outlines unless deliberately stylized. The creature should feel airy and luminous.

Color and Lighting

The palette should support a relaxing ambient mood.

Recommended palette direction

Base colors:

deep indigo

violet

cyan

soft magenta

pale blue-white highlights

The body can have a layered luminous look with cool tones dominant and warmer accent flickers in small amounts.

Glow behavior

Glow should be restrained:

soft outer glow around bell

faint emission along tentacles

occasional brighter node points or pulses along strands

nothing flashy, strobing, or nightclub-adjacent

This module is supposed to calm the room down, not audition for a Berlin techno flyer.

Transparency

Use transparency heavily:

the bell should feel semi-translucent

overlapping layers should create soft depth

tentacles should fade as they extend

some peripheral strands can nearly disappear into the background

That semi-ethereal transparency is important for making it feel non-intrusive.

Emotional Tone

This module should feel like:

a living desktop terrarium

a slow alien creature in a station viewport

a meditative ambient system ornament

something between scientific visualization and dream imagery

It should encourage the user to glance over and breathe for a second. A micro-dose of calm.

No urgency. No “game object” energy. No obvious loopiness.

Suggested Structure of the Creature

A nice composition would be:

1. Main bell

Large, centered, slightly asymmetrical so it feels organic.

2. Inner core

A glowing nucleus or organ cluster visible through the bell. This can pulse faintly with the swim cycle.

3. Mid-length oral arms

Thicker ribbon-like appendages closer to the body, curling and undulating slowly.

4. Long trailing tentacles

Several major strands extending well beyond one monitor, with graceful variation in length.

5. Fine sensory filaments

Very thin strands drifting more independently and adding delicacy.

This layered anatomy prevents it from looking like a generic umbrella with spaghetti.

Motion Design Principles

To keep the effect relaxing, motion should obey a few rules:

Rule 1: No sudden acceleration

Changes in direction and speed should be smoothed with easing curves.

Rule 2: No mechanical looping

Add slight timing variance, phase offsets, and low-amplitude random drift so the motion doesn’t repeat too obviously.

Rule 3: Movement should originate from the bell

The contraction of the bell drives the rest of the body. Tentacles respond afterward with lag.

Rule 4: Peripheral motion should continue

Even when the main body is nearly still, tiny motion in the tentacles keeps it alive.

Rule 5: Keep amplitude low

All animation should stay subtle enough that it doesn’t distract from actual work on the monitors.

Background Integration

The jellyfish can work over a dark background or semi-transparent overlay.

Best-case visual context

It would look especially strong against:

dark space-themed wallpapers

starfields

black or near-black backgrounds

subtle nebula gradients

dim UI environments

Non-intrusive overlay

If it sits above normal desktop content, opacity should be tuned so it remains visible without interfering with readability. Tentacles can be lighter and more transparent in busy areas.

The creature should feel like an ambient layer passing through the workspace, not vandalizing it.

Optional Ambient Enhancements

These are nice extras if you want the module to feel even more alive.

Soft particle motes

Tiny drifting specks around the jellyfish, like cosmic plankton. Very sparse.

Faint pulse along tentacles

A slow-moving glow traveling down a strand every so often, like bioluminescent energy flow.

Micro-parallax

Inner layers of the bell can shift very slightly relative to one another during movement, adding depth.

Rare posture changes

Every once in a while, one tentacle curls differently or the bell tilts a little more than usual. Small variations help prevent “screensaver repetition syndrome.”

Scale Recommendations

The creature should be big.

Not absurdly huge, but big enough to own space across the monitor wall.

A good visual target:

bell occupies perhaps 20–40% of one display height

longest tentacles reach well into adjacent displays

total full-body footprint can span 1.5 to 3 monitors, depending on layout

This is what makes it feel like an environmental presence rather than a sticker.

Silhouette and Readability

Even with transparency and glow, the shape should be readable at a glance:

the bell should have a clean recognizable jellyfish silhouette

tentacles should create elegant downward rhythm

the whole outline should remain attractive whether the creature is centered or partially offscreen

A strong silhouette matters because users will often catch it from the corner of their eye.

Technical Behavior Summary

If this is being handed off as a module concept, the core behavior is:

vector-rendered space jellyfish

calm looping ambient animation

slow vertical float and gentle pulse

tentacles with delayed secondary motion

multi-monitor coordinate awareness

long appendages spanning more than one display

low visual distraction

soothing, luminous, cosmic tone

One Good Artistic Direction Sentence

If you want a single-line art brief for a designer or code assistant:

Create a large, semi-transparent bioluminescent space jellyfish in vector graphics that drifts slowly up and down across a multi-monitor desktop, with long graceful tentacles spanning adjacent screens and motion calm enough to feel meditative rather than attention-seeking.
